DISPOSITION

EXAMPLE

DO_NOT_CALL

 

  • Machine answers and says “Do not call this number.”
  • Someone pranks or trolls you.
  • “Take me off your list”
  • “I’m not interested, goodbye”

OTHER

  • Voter is deceased
  • Any other option not covered in the guide

WRONG NUMBER

  • Voicemail, definitely not the person
  • Fax Machine
  • “You have the wrong number.”
  • You contact a business and not the correct person
  • You hear “This Phone Number has been disconnected”

BOT

  • A bot (recording) answers
  • A “bot detection” service prompts you to enter numbers to verify you are a person and the call does not go through after entering numbers

OTHER LANGUAGE

  • Caller does not speak English

CALL DISCONNECTED

  • Hang up before you know who it is
  • Greeted by a generic voicemail response (DO NOT LEAVE A VOICEMAIL)
  • The dialer just keeps ringing and no one answers
  • Person hangs up after you say “I’m a volunteer with the Bernie campaign”

NOT HOME/BUSY

  • “Person is not home right now.”
  • or “Bernard is busy right now.”
  • or “Bernard can’t talk right now.”
  • Person says: “Hi, this is me. I’m busy right now.”

MOVED

  • “Person doesn’t live here anymore/moved.”
  • or "Person is away at college."
  • Person says “I moved to another state”

5- Will Not Support Bernie

  • “I would never vote for Bernie”
  • “I’m voting for the Opponent”

4 - Unlikely to Support Bernie

  • “I’m probably for voting for the Opponent”

3- Undecided

  • “I still haven’t decided”

2- Will Likely Support Bernie

  • “I’ll probably vote for Bernie”

1- Strong Bernie Supporter

  • “I am voting for Bernie!”

Already Voted

  • Select this regardless of which candidate they voted for.

Not Voting

  • “I am not eligible to vote”
  • “I will be out of town that day”
  • “I don't vote in primaries”

Answering Machine

  • Voicemail prompt has person's name

We encourage supporters with particular health concerns about voting at a polling place to consult with your doctor and take all the necessary precautions laid out by the CDC and Health Departments. Your health is most important! At this moment, the states that are scheduled to vote on March 17 are currently proceeding with elections.

The elections officials of Arizona, Florida, Illinois, and Ohio put out the following statement on Friday, March 13:

"As each of our four states prepare for voters to head to the polls on Tuesday, March 17, 2020, we are working closely with our state health officials to ensure that our poll workers and voters can be confident that voting is safe. Unlike concerts, sporting events, or other mass gatherings where large groups of people travel long distances to congregate in a confined space for an extended period of time, polling locations see people from a nearby community coming into and out of the building for a short duration. Further, guidance from voting machine manufacturers on how best to sanitize machines, guidelines from CDC on best practices for hand washing, and guidance from our respective state health officials is being provided to every polling location.Americans have participated in elections during challenging times in the past, and based on the best information we have from public health officials, we are confident that voters in our states can safely and securely cast their ballots in this election, and that otherwise healthy poll workers can and should carry out their patriotic duties on Tuesday."
